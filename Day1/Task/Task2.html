Group Captain Abhinandan Varthaman

Born: June 21, 1983 (age 41)
Allegiance: India
Service Branch: Indian Air Force
Years of Service: 2004–present
Rank: Group Captain
Service Number: 27981
Battles/Wars:

  • 2019 India–Pakistan Standoff
  • 2019 Jammu and Kashmir Airstrikes (POW)

Awards: Vir Chakra

Biography

Group Captain Abhinandan Varthaman VrC (born 21 June 1983) is an Indian Air Force fighter pilot of the Mig-21 Bison aircraft. On 27 February 2019, he scrambled to intercept F-16 and JF-17 jets of the Pakistan Air Force conducting retaliatory airstrikes on Jammu and Kashmir. He was awarded the Vir Chakra by the Indian government for his bravery. He was captured when his aircraft was shot down inside Pakistani territory, just 7 km (4.3 miles) from the Line of Control, and was repatriated on 1 March 2019.



Career and personal life

Abhinandan Varthaman was born on 21 June 1983 in a Tamil Jain family. Varthaman's family is from Thirupanamoor, a village about 19 km (12 miles) from Kanchipuram. His father Simhakutty Varthaman is retired Air Marshal (IAF), his wife Tanvi Marwaha is also a retired Squadron Leader (IAF) and his mother is a doctor. Varthaman was educated at Sainik School, Amaravathinagar. He graduated from the National Defence Academy and was commissioned into the combat (fighter) stream of the IAF as a flying officer on 19 June 2004.He was trained at the IAF centres in Bathinda and Halwara, promoted to flight lieutenant on 19 June 2006, and to squadron leader on 8 July 2010. Varthaman was a Su-30MKI fighter pilot before being assigned to the MiG-21 Bison squadron.He was promoted to wing commander on 19 June 2017 and to group captain in November 2021. Varthaman is married and lives in Chennai. He and his wife have two children.

Balakot Airstrike

The Balakot Airstrike was a significant military action conducted by the Indian Air Force on February 26, 2019. This operation was in response to the Pulwama attack that occurred on February 14, 2019, where a suicide bomber targeted an Indian paramilitary convoy, killing 40 personnel.

The Indian government blamed the Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM), a Pakistan-based militant group, for the Pulwama attack. As a retaliatory measure, Indian fighter jets carried out airstrikes on a JeM training camp located in Balakot, Pakistan.

In the early hours of February 26, Indian Mirage 2000 fighter jets crossed the Line of Control (LoC) and bombed the target site at Balakot. The operation lasted for a few minutes and was hailed as a pre-emptive strike to neutralize terrorist threats.

The Indian government claimed that several militants, including high-profile commanders of Jaish-e-Mohammed, were killed in the strike. However, Pakistan disputed this, stating that no casualties occurred and the Indian jets had only damaged forest areas. The international community, including the United Nations, urged both countries to show restraint.
